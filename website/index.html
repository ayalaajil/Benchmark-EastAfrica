What this is

A self-contained, reproducible benchmark of machine-learning weather models on daily total rainfall over East Africa (12°S–15°N, 28°E–52°E, 1° grid, land cells only). Every figure and table on this site is produced by one command, run_verification.py, from publicly available inputs.

Protocol — MAM 2024

Each model is initialized daily at 00 UTC from ERA5 analyses over the March–May 2024 long-rains season (92 initializations, 1 Mar – 31 May) and verified at lead days 1, 3, 5 and 7: a forecast started on day t is scored against observations valid on t+ℓ. Daily totals are formed on each model's native cadence, then conservatively regridded to the common 1° grid (mm day⁻¹).

References

Every model is verified against three independent rainfall products — CHIRPS v2.0 (primary, gauge–satellite), ERA5 (reanalysis) and TAMSAT v3.1 (satellite) — so conclusions are not artifacts of a single observational product.

Metrics in brief

Bias / MAE / RMSE — errors of the ensemble mean.  ACC — anomaly correlation w.r.t. the truth's seasonal mean.  CRPS — probabilistic error of the full ensemble (fair estimator).  Spread/RMSE (SSR) — dispersion calibration; 1 = well calibrated.  Rank histogram — flat = calibrated; tie-aware ranks (Hamill 2001).  CRPSS — skill relative to the CHIRPS climatology; >0 beats it.
